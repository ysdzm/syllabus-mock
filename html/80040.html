授業の目的・達成目標

This is a student oriented seminar style class. The emphasis is on the content of the course and not on teaching or learning English skills per se.

 

This is a seminar style class. Students will read and discuss the book, The Idea Factory by Jon Gertner. Students will be expected to read the book before class begins and each student will have the opportunity to summarize and make a brief presentation of the important ideas in a chapter. The student will then lead that class's discussion.

授業計画

 

week 1

Introduction

Explain the format of the class, do some   introduction activities and explain the homework to research: Mervin Kelley, William   Shockley, John Bardeen, William Brattain, and Jack Morton

week 2

The   Role of personal history in innovation

Discuss the people researched in the   previous week and illuminate how their personal background led them to Bell   Labs.

week 3

Innovation leaps or crawls

The development of the intercontinental   phone system took years and advanced incrementally. The transistor   transformed this system. What factors encouraged or discouraged its adoption.

week 4

Government and innovation

What is the role of the government in   supporting innovation? How did the government support Bell Labs? Is such   support necessary or ultimately harmful?

week 5

Innovation by design

When Bell Labs moved to their new   research facility it was designed to encourage collaboration and innovation. How   effective are such design features? Which are necessary? Which could be done   away with?

week 6

Innovation by individual or by   collaboration

Everyone at Bell Labs was very   intelligent. What factors pushed some of these people to be able to develop great   innovations? Was it the environment at Bell Labs or was it their individual   genius.

week 7

The future

The current Internet is built on a   backbone developed by telephone companies over a 100 years ago. Where is the   innovation? We were promised flying cars. We got 144 characters. Is   innovation over?

week 8

Final discussion

Write a brief essay explaining your   opinion about some of the issues raised in the class.
