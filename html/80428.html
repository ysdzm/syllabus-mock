授業の目的・達成目標

The class develops and applies mechanical engineering disciplines, particularly continuum mechanics, to biomechanical phenomena over a range of length scales, from cellular to tissue or organ level. It is intended for intermediate graduate students who have been exposed to vectors and tensors, differential equations, fluids or fields or transport, and certain aspects of biology and physiology. Most of the material will come from journal articles and notes to be handed in the class.

 

授業計画

 

The anticipated schedule of classroom activities is shown below. Letters, papers and news topics that are related to biophysics are assigned to students. In each class, the assigned students are responsible for making a presentation for 30 minutes to explain the content of papers, topics and problems in English, which is followed by class discussion that is also done in English. Your active participation is expected and required! Class participation is awarded based on your contribution to discussion. The presenter need create a written summary of the presentation for the class, with touching on the main points of your presentation and including explanations of technical terms, theories, technologies and so forth used in the presentation. However even if you are not the presenter of the day, you are still responsible for doing the reading in advance of class, and taking an active role in class activities and discussion. Discussions will vary in format (e.g., small group discussion, activities, classroom demonstrations).

 

1. Introduction (Syllabus and overview)

2. Cellular Biomechanics

3. Hemodynamics in large arteries

4. Hemodynamics in small arteries

5. Circulatory System

6. Interstitium

7. Ocular Biomechanics

8. Respiratory System
